Definition

What is freedom time?

Freedom time is the number of years your current portfolio could carry your current lifestyle without earning. Freedom Clock turns that slightly strange, useful question into a calculator and an e-ink desk device.

The number moves. That is the point — not a static net worth figure, but a live answer to a human question.

The definition

Freedom time is a personal finance unit of measure, but less sterile than that sounds. Instead of asking "what is my net worth?", it asks "how much time has that net worth already bought?"

The number depends on your assets, expected growth, inflation, income, spending, and whether you keep investing. If the portfolio can cover spending indefinitely after inflation, Freedom Clock shows FOREVER.

The math

At the simplest level, the calculator grows each asset, subtracts the gap between monthly spend and monthly income, adjusts for inflation, then does it again. The result is the point where the portfolio runs out, expressed in years and months.

Bitcoin-aware scenarios add borrow rate and collateral assumptions. That is why the calculator includes sell monthly, borrow, and borrow-then-sell modes. A stack you can borrow against behaves differently from a savings account.

Lifetime left

Lifetime left is separate from money. It estimates the time between your current age and the life expectancy you enter. It is not a prediction. It is a reminder that money is only useful inside a life.

Freedom coverage

Freedom coverage compares freedom time with lifetime left. If you have 10 years of freedom time and 40 years of expected life left, coverage is 25%. A small number, maybe. But it moves.
