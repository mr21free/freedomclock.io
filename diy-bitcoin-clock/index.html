What is a bitcoin clock?

A bitcoin clock is a small dedicated desk device that keeps one piece of Bitcoin-related data visible at a glance — block height, current BTC price in dollars, sats per dollar, or some combination. The appeal is having a quiet physical thing that does one job: no phone, no browser tab, no notifications.

The best-known commercial bitcoin clock is the BLOCKCLOCK, made by Coinkite. If you are looking for a DIY bitcoin clock or a BLOCKCLOCK alternative, this page covers both sides honestly.

BLOCKCLOCK: the reference product

Coinkite currently sells two BLOCKCLOCK models. Both are Wi-Fi connected, require an internet connection to display live data, and ship with firmware distributed as signed binaries. Source code is not published.

BLOCKCLOCK mini — $399

Seven E Ink digit displays on a 30×10 cm panel. Shows BTC price, block height, Moscow time, sats per dollar, and can display an Opendime balance via USB. Verified price: $399 at coinkite.com (June 2026).

BLOCKCLOCK micro — $189

Single e-ink display, 11×7 cm. Shows BTC price, block height, Moscow time, sats per dollar, and MSTR tracking. Verified price: $189 at coinkite.com (June 2026).

If you want live block height and BTC price in a finished, polished object, a BLOCKCLOCK is the right answer. Buy one.

The DIY path

Freedom Clock is an open-source ESP32 e-ink desk device. The firmware is MIT licensed, the hardware is off-the-shelf, and the total cost is around $30 in parts.

What it shows

Freedom time: the number of years and months your savings, Bitcoin, and other assets could support your current spending without new earned income. In BTC mode, it also fetches a live BTC price from CoinGecko — no API key, no account. The question it answers is not "what is Bitcoin worth right now?" but "how much time have your savings already bought?"

Hardware

A Heltec Vision Master E290: a single board with a 2.90-inch e-ink display, Wi-Fi, USB-C port, and battery charging circuit built in. Add a 3.7 V Li-Po battery and a USB-C cable. Total around $30. No soldering. The board is available from Heltec and electronics suppliers.

Setup

Install the Arduino IDE, open Freedom_Clock.ino from the GitHub repository, connect the board via USB-C, and click upload. After upload the device broadcasts a setup Wi-Fi network. Join from your phone or laptop and enter your home Wi-Fi and financial settings. First refresh happens within minutes. The full build guide covers every step.

Customisation

The firmware is MIT licensed. Every file is readable. Display layout lives in display_screens.h. Quote library in quotes.h. Adding a new data mode means adding a screen definition and wiring it to a data source — the build guide links to the relevant files.

FAQ

Can the Freedom Clock show live BTC price?

Yes. In BTC mode the device fetches a live price from CoinGecko. No API key or account is required.

Does it show block height?

Not in the current firmware. The device focuses on freedom time and BTC price. If you specifically need live block height on your desk, a BLOCKCLOCK is the better fit.

Do I need to solder anything?

No. The Heltec Vision Master E290 has the display, Wi-Fi, USB-C, and battery charging built into one board. The battery clips onto a standard connector. The only tools you need are a computer and a USB-C cable.

Can I reskin or modify the display?

Yes. The firmware is MIT licensed and the full source is on GitHub. Display layout lives in display_screens.h. You can change what is shown, adjust the layout, or add new data modes.

Is this a BLOCKCLOCK alternative?

It depends on what you need. If you want live block height and current BTC price in a polished finished device, BLOCKCLOCK is good hardware — buy one. Freedom Clock answers a different question: not what Bitcoin is worth right now, but how many years of your life your savings have already bought. Different device, different question.
